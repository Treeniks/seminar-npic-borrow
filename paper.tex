\begin{abstract}
In recent years, beaches have gained an increasing importance in the selection of vacation places. However, the lack of beach-readiness of conventional computers prevented programmers from working during their holidays. While the advent of beach computers provides programmers with suitable hardware devices, the lack of proper support in the CPU's instruction set remains. Therefore, in this paper, we propose a novel beach mode for x86 that provides alternative beach-related semantics to the conventional instructions. Our new mode is binary-compatible, so that existing programs can transparently execute with the beach-optimized semantics without requiring re-compilation. The beachmark results indicate an increase in fun by 2.43x at the expense of some computational correctness.
\end{abstract}
